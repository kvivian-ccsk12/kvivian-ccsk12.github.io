Special Rules

Castling

Building a Fort for Your King

Notes:

  • You cannot castle while in check, through check, or into check.
  • You can only castle if the King and rook you're castling with haven't moved.
  • The King only ever moves two squares.
  • Tournament Rule: Touch your King first when castling.

Pawn Promotion

Reaching the Endzone

Notes:

  • A pawn can become any piece (aside from a King) when it reaches the end.
  • Most of the time you're going to want to make a Queen.
  • The piece doesn't have to already have been captured. Make 9 Queens if you can!

Attacking and Defending

Piece Value

Know Their Worth

Piece Approximate Value
Pawn1
Knight3
Bishop3
Rook5
Queen9

Notes:

  • Pieces are only as good as they're placed.
  • Knights and Bishops are both 'worth' 3, but typically bishops are preferred.
  • Pawns get more valuable the closer they are to promotion.

Defended and Undefended Pieces

The Land of the Free (Pieces)

Notes:

  • Check if you can take any of your opponent's pieces for free.
  • Always check if the place you're moving to is attacked.
  • Always check if moving your piece undefends another one of your pieces.

Counting

Knowing When (and When Not) to Trade

Notes:

  • Check how many attackers vs. defenders are on a piece.
  • If there are more attackers, then the piece might be able to be captured (conditions apply, see the board for details).
  • If the number is equal or there are more defenders, then the piece probably shouldn't be captured.

Trapped Pieces

When There's No Way Out

Notes:

  • Pieces with no way to move out of an attack may be free pieces.
  • Generally, pieces deep in enemy territory are the ones that get trapped.
  • To avoid getting your pieces trapped, try to make sure that there is a retreating square for pieces near the opponent.
