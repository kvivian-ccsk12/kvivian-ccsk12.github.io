Checkmating with a Queen

The 'Knight Move' Mate

Notes:

  • Keep the Queen a knight's move away from the King and gradually force him to the corner of the board.
  • Make sure to give him two squares to move between once he is at the corner.
  • Bring your King close and deliver a checkmate with the King protecting the Queen.

Checkmating with Two Rooks

The 'Ladder' Mate

Notes:

  • Set up your rooks on different ranks and files.
  • Use one rook as a force-field while the other checks the opponent's King.
  • Move the rooks out of the way if attacked by the King.
  • Repeat until checkmate.

Checkmating with a Rook

The 'Shrinking Square' Mate

Notes:

  • Keep your King protecting your rook.
  • Take every opportunity to shrink the opponent's King's squares.
  • When the opponent's King is on the side of the board, move the rook away, get the Kings facing each other and deliver checkmate.

Planning

Activating your Pieces

Notes:

  • Games are often decided on whose pieces are more 'active'.
  • Control squares and attack your opponent's pieces.
  • Move your pieces to places where they do this.
  • Typically pieces in the center are better than at the edge.

Notes:

  • Pawns are like doors and walls for pieces; they open or close pieces out.
  • Knights like to find 'gaps' in enemy pawns to jump into (outpost).
  • Bishops don't like being stuck behind pawns.
  • Rooks like open files, and they really like the 7th rank.
  • Rooks can also be activated by a 'rook lift'; moving it up and over to attack.

THE MOST IMPORTANT CHECKLIST

The 3 things to look for:

  1. Checks
  2. Captures
  3. Attacks (focus on Queen for now)

These are typically called 'forcing moves'.

Notes:

  • Go through this checklist for every move.
  • Go through this checklist from your opponent's perspective too.
  • You don't have to play the move, but you should see the move.
