Introducing the Pieces

The King

Your most important piece

Notes:

  • Slow and fragile, but absolutely must be protected.
  • It's the only piece that you cannot let be captured.
  • It starts to be much more useful toward the end of the game.

The Pawn

Your frontline fighters

Notes:

  • You cannot 'save' your first move boost. Use it or lose it!
  • Pawns are the only piece that captures different than it moves.
  • Pawns like to be together, protecting one another.

The Knight

The tricky horse

Notes:

  • Knights are the only ones who can jump over other pieces (like a horse).
  • Knights flip the colored square they are on each move.
  • Knights are best in the middle of the board where they can control the most squares.

The Bishop

The diagonal sniper

Notes:

  • Bishops move diagonally as far as they want (but not over things). This keeps them on the same color.
  • Placing Bishops in the center makes more use of them, but it can also be good to put them in a corner to 'snipe' your opponent.
  • You have two bishops to control both colored squares. Try and keep them or you have less control of half the board!

The Rook

The tower of power

Notes:

  • Rooks move in straight lines as far as they want.
  • Rooks like looking at each other to double their power and protect each other.
  • Rooks take a while to come to the battle, but are very powerful toward the end of the game.

The Queen

The almighty piece

Notes:

  • The Queen moves like a rook and a bishop combined.
  • The Queen is your most powerful piece. If you lose it the game will be very difficult to win.
  • The Queen is a team player; it really likes working with the other pieces to attack the opponent.

How to Play

Check

The forceful attack

When a piece is attacking the King, it is said to be 'in check'

Rules of Check

  • If you are in check, you must get out of check.
  • You cannot put yourself into check.
  • If you put your opponent in check, quietly and politely tell them they are in check.

The last one isn't an official rule, but we will be doing it in this camp to help us learn.

Getting out of Check

  1. Capturing the checking piece.
  2. Blocking the check.
  3. Moving the King.

Checkmate

The end of the game

When a King cannot get out of check then he is said to be in 'checkmate'. This is the point where the game is over.

Setting up your Board

Things to remember:

  • "White on the right" - The bottom right square should be a light square.
  • "King on the opposite color" - White King will go on a dark square, Black King on a light square.
  • Bishop is almost royal - Put the bishops next to the King and Queen.
